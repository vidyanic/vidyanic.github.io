## üéØ The Four Types of Moksha

Different traditions describe slightly different "flavors":

| Type | Sanskrit | Description |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **Living in God's realm** | Salokya | Permanent residence in divine Loka |
| **Being near God** | Samipya | Eternal closeness to the divine |
| **Having God's form** | Sarupya | Taking on divine attributes |
| **Merging with God** | Sayujya | Complete union, no separation |

**Advaita (non-dual) view:** All these are stages; final Moksha is realizing there was never anyone separate to be liberated.

## üöß What Blocks Moksha?

Since Moksha is your natural state, nothing needs to be "attained."

What blocks it?

| Block | Sanskrit | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Ignorance** | Avidya | Not knowing your true nature |
| **Ego** | Ahamkara | Identification with body/mind |
| **Attachment** | Raga | Clinging to pleasure |
| **Aversion** | Dvesha | Running from pain |
| **Clinging to life** | Abhinivesha | Fear of death |

Remove these, and Moksha is self-evident.

## ‚ùì Common Questions

### "Does Moksha mean I die?"

No. The body may continue. Life continues. But YOU no longer take yourself to be the body. Death becomes like taking off a costume.

### "Do I lose my personality?"

The limited ego dissolves. But you don't become a zombie. Liberated beings often have more personality, more creativity, more life ‚Äî just without the suffering.

### "Can anyone achieve Moksha?"

Yes. It's your birthright. Some may be closer than others, but no one is excluded.

### "How long does it take?"

Depends on:
- Starting point (Samskaras)
- Intensity of practice
- Grace (Guru, Divine)
- Could be lifetimes. Could be this moment.

### "Is it permanent?"

Once realized fully, yes. You can't unknow what you know.
