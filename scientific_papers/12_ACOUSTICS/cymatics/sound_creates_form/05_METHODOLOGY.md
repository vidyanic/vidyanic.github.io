# 05. Methodology: Experimental Approach

## 5.1 Research Design

### 5.1.1 Approach

This study employs:
1. **Experimental replication:** Reproduce classic cymatics experiments
2. **Pattern documentation:** Catalog frequency-pattern relationships
3. **Comparative analysis:** Match cymatic patterns to natural forms
4. **Literature correlation:** Align findings with Vedic claims
5. **Validation testing:** Apply 7-concept stack

---

## 5.2 Experimental Methods

### 5.2.1 Chladni Plate Method

**Equipment:**
- Metal plate (square or circular)
- Speaker/driver connected to plate center
- Frequency generator (signal source)
- Fine sand or powder
- Camera for documentation

**Procedure:**
1. Sprinkle sand evenly on plate
2. Apply sine wave at specific frequency
3. Observe sand movement to nodes
4. Document resulting pattern
5. Repeat at different frequencies

### 5.2.2 Water Cymatics

**Equipment:**
- Shallow dish of water
- Speaker beneath dish
- Frequency generator
- High-speed camera
- Good lighting

**Procedure:**
1. Fill dish with water
2. Apply frequency from below
3. Observe surface wave patterns
4. Photograph at peak formation
5. Catalog by frequency

### 5.2.3 CymaScope Method

**Equipment:**
- Specialized cymatics visualization device
- Water chamber
- LED lighting
- High-resolution camera

**Procedure:**
1. Apply sound/voice to chamber
2. Capture high-resolution image
3. Analyze pattern geometry
4. Compare across frequencies

## 5.6 Limitations

### 5.6.1 Acknowledged Limitations

1. **Medium dependence:** Different media may show variation
2. **Amplitude effects:** Amplitude affects pattern clarity
3. **Equipment variation:** Different setups may vary slightly
4. **Biological inference:** Natural form resemblance is not proof of mechanism

### 5.6.2 Mitigation

1. Use standardized equipment
2. Control amplitude carefully
3. Document all conditions
4. Treat biological inference as hypothesis, not conclusion
