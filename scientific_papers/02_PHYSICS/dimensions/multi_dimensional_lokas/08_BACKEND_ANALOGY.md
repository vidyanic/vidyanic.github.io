## 8.2 Guna-Loka Relationship

> **"ऊर्ध्वं गच्छन्ति सत्त्वस्था"**
> "Those established in Sattva go upward."
> — Bhagavad Gita 14.18

### 8.2.1 The Principle

```
SATTVA -> Upper Lokas (lighter, brighter, faster time)
RAJAS -> Middle (Bhu) (mixed, active)
TAMAS -> Lower Lokas (denser, darker, slower time)

At death, Jiva goes to Loka matching its Guna state:
• Sattvic person -> Higher Lokas
• Rajasic person -> Rebirth on Earth
• Tamasic person -> Lower Lokas
```

### 8.2.2 Guna Proportions by Loka

```
SATYA:  S=99%  -> Pure consciousness, near Moksha
BHU:    S=33%  -> Physical, mixed, action possible
PATALA: T=95%  -> Dense, slow, transformation needed
```

## 8.3 Time Dilation in Lokas

> **"त्रैलोक्ये यो दिने राजन् षष्ठो भागः स उच्यते"**
> "What is one day in the three worlds is said to be one-sixth..."
> — Bhagavata Purana

### 8.3.1 Time Scaling

```
Brahma-Loka: 1 day = 4.32 billion Earth years
Svarga-Loka: 1 day = 1 Earth year
Bhu-Loka: Normal time
Lower Lokas: Time runs slower

INTERPRETATION:
Higher Sattva -> Higher frequency -> Faster time (relative)
Higher Tamas -> Lower frequency -> Slower time (relative)
```

### 8.3.2 The Revati Story

```
King Kakudmi took daughter Revati to Brahma-Loka
Spent "moments" there
Returned to find 27 Chaturyugas (116 million years) passed

INTERPRETATION:
• Travel between Lokas = Frequency shift
• Time runs at different rates
• EXACTLY matches relativistic time dilation
```
