## 8.1 Shabda-Brahman in Backend Texts

### 8.1.1 Sound as Brahman

> **"शब्दब्रह्मणि निष्णातः परं ब्रह्माधिगच्छति"**
> "One skilled in Shabda-Brahman attains the Supreme Brahman."
> — Brahma Bindu Upanishad

Shabda-Brahman means:
- Reality IS sound/vibration
- Brahman manifests AS sound
- Understanding sound = Understanding reality
- Mastery of sound = Mastery of creation

### 8.1.2 Nada-Brahman

> **"नादब्रह्म परमं ज्योतिः"**
> "Nada-Brahman is the supreme light."
> — Nada Bindu Upanishad

Nada (नाद) = Primordial sound
- Before manifestation
- The first vibration
- Source of all creation

## 8.4 Nada Yoga

### 8.4.1 The Practice

> **"नादानुसन्धानं समाधिलक्षणम्"**
> "Absorption in Nada is the mark of Samadhi."
> — Hatha Yoga Pradipika 4.66

Nada Yoga = Union through sound:
1. Sit in meditation
2. Close ears (or use internal focus)
3. Listen for inner sound (Anahata Nada)
4. Follow sound to source
5. Merge with sound -> Samadhi

### 8.4.2 Stages of Nada

| Stage | Sound | Level |
|-------|-------|-------|
| 1 | Ocean roar, bells | Gross |
| 2 | Flute, vina | Subtle |
| 3 | Buzzing, humming | Very subtle |
| 4 | Silence (Nada source) | Para Nada |

## 8.6 OM: The Primordial Mantra

### 8.6.1 Structure of OM

> **"ओंकार एवेदं सर्वं"**
> "OM is indeed all this."
> — Mandukya Upanishad 1

```
OM = A + U + M + SILENCE:
=========================

A (अ) = CREATION
• Opening of mouth
• Beginning of vibration
• Brahma principle
• Waking state (Jagrat)

U (उ) = PRESERVATION
• Middle position
• Sustaining vibration
• Vishnu principle
• Dream state (Swapna)

M (म) = DISSOLUTION
• Closing of mouth
• Ending vibration
• Shiva principle
• Deep sleep (Sushupti)

SILENCE = TURIYA
• Beyond the three
• Pure witness
• Brahman itself
• Fourth state
```
