# 2. LITERATURE REVIEW

## 2.1 Music Therapy Research

### 2.1.1 Pain Reduction

**Cepeda et al. (2006):** Meta-analysis of music for pain:
- Pain reduction in 51 studies
- Average effect: 0.4 SD reduction
- Works for post-operative, chronic, and cancer pain

**Good et al. (2001):** Music for post-surgical pain:
- 24% less pain medication needed
- Significantly lower anxiety scores

### 2.1.2 Stress and Anxiety

**Bradt & Dileo (2014):** Music for stress reduction:
- Significant reduction in anxiety
- Cortisol decrease documented
- Heart rate variability improvement

### 2.1.3 Neurological Effects

**Särkämö et al. (2008):** Music for stroke recovery:
- Improved cognitive recovery
- Better mood outcomes
- Enhanced verbal memory

## 2.2 Cymatics Research

### 2.2.1 Hans Jenny (1967-1972)

Documented relationship between sound and form:
- "Cymatics: A Study of Wave Phenomena"
- Thousands of photographs of sound patterns
- Demonstrated: specific frequency -> specific pattern

### 2.2.2 Alexander Lauterwasser

Extended Jenny's work:
- Water sound images
- Complex harmonic patterns
- Living forms resembling organic structures

### 2.2.3 John Stuart Reid

CymaScope device:
- High-resolution sound visualization
- Documented voice patterns
- Analyzed music and mantra

## 2.3 Frequency-Specific Research

- Limited but growing research

### 2.3.2 432 Hz vs 440 Hz

| Aspect | 432 Hz | 440 Hz |
|--------|--------|--------|
| History | Ancient standard | Modern standard (1939) |

### 2.3.3 Binaural Beats

**Wahbeh et al. (2007):** Review of binaural beats:
- Some effect on anxiety

## 2.5 Traditional Sound Practices

### 2.5.1 OM Chanting

**Kalyani et al. (2011):** OM vs control sound:
- Limbic deactivation with OM
- Not with control sound
- Vagal tone improvement

**Gurjar & Ladhake (2008):** OM waveform analysis:
- Specific frequency signature
- Approximately 136.1 Hz
- Matches Earth orbital frequency

### 2.5.2 Tibetan Singing Bowls

**Goldsby et al. (2017):** Singing bowl meditation:
- Decreased tension, anger, fatigue
- Increased spiritual well-being
- Preliminary evidence

### 2.5.3 Mantra

**Various studies:**
- Repetitive sound reduces stress
- Specific mantras show specific effects
- Mechanism unclear
