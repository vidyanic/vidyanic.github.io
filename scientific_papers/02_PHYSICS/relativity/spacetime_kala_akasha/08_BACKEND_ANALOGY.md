## 8.3 Jyoti-Gati (Light Speed) in Backend

> **"योजनानां सहस्रे द्वे द्वे शते द्वे च योजने। एकेन निमिषार्धेन क्रममाण नमोऽस्तु ते॥"**
> "O Sun, you who traverse 2,202 yojanas in half a nimesha, salutations to you."
> — Sayana's Rig Veda Commentary (14th century)

### 8.3.1 Calculation

```
VEDIC CALCULATION:
==================

Distance: 2,202 yojanas
Time: 0.5 nimesha (half-blink)

Convert:
1 yojana = ~13 km (variable)
1 nimesha = 16/75 second

Speed = (2,202 × 13,000) / (16/150)
      = 28,626,000 m / 0.107 s
      ≈ 2.68 × 10⁸ m/s

MODERN c = 2.998 × 10⁸ m/s

Accuracy: ~89% (remarkable for ancient calculation)
```

## 8.4 Time Dilation in Backend

### 8.4.1 Different Lokas, Different Time

```
LOKA TIME SCALING:
==================

| Loka | Time Relative to Earth |
|------|------------------------|
| Brahmaloka | 1 Brahma day = 4.32 billion Earth years |
| Svargaloka | 1 Deva day = 1 human year |
| Bhuloka | Standard time |
| Patalaloka | Different scaling |

INTERPRETATION:
• Higher Lokas = faster processing (Sattva dominant)
• Lower Lokas = slower processing (Tamas dominant)
• Time rate depends on Guna composition
• EXACTLY what relativity predicts for gravitational time dilation
```

### 8.4.2 Revati Story (Time Dilation Account)

```
FROM BHAGAVATA PURANA:
======================

King Kakudmi took daughter Revati to Brahmaloka
Spent "moments" there
Returned to find 27 Chaturyugas passed (116 million years)

INTERPRETATION:
• Travel to higher Loka = gravitational time dilation
• Less Tamas = faster time (in that frame)
• Return shows differential aging
• Matches twin paradox in relativity
```
