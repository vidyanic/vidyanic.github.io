## 2.1 Thermodynamics Foundations

### 2.1.1 Historical Development

| Year | Scientist | Contribution |
|------|-----------|--------------|
| 1824 | Carnot | Heat engine efficiency limits |

| 1865 | Clausius | Named "entropy" |
| 1877 | Boltzmann | Statistical interpretation |
| 1948 | Shannon | Information entropy |
| 2003 | Jarzynski | Fluctuation theorems |

### 2.1.2 Key Papers

**Clausius, R. (1865).** "Über verschiedene für die Anwendung bequeme Formen der Hauptgleichungen der mechanischen Wärmetheorie." *Annalen der Physik*, 201(7), 353-400.
- Introduced the term "entropy"
- Formulated: dS ≥ δQ/T

**Boltzmann, L. (1877).** "Über die Beziehung zwischen dem zweiten Hauptsatze der mechanischen Wärmetheorie und der Wahrscheinlichkeitsrechnung." *Sitzungsberichte der Akademie der Wissenschaften*, 76, 373-435.
- Statistical interpretation: S = k·ln(W)
- Connected entropy to probability

**Shannon, C. (1948).** "A Mathematical Theory of Communication." *Bell System Technical Journal*, 27(3), 379-423.
- Information entropy: H = -Σ p·log(p)
- Entropy as missing information

## 2.2 Modern Developments

### 2.2.1 Non-Equilibrium Thermodynamics

**Prigogine, I. (1977).** "Self-Organization in Non-Equilibrium Systems."
- Dissipative structures
- Order from chaos (at entropy cost)

### 2.2.2 Fluctuation Theorems

**Jarzynski, C. (1997).** "Nonequilibrium Equality for Free Energy Differences." *Physical Review Letters*, 78, 2690.
- Entropy can decrease temporarily
- But Second Law holds statistically

### 2.2.3 Black Hole Thermodynamics

**Bekenstein, J. (1973).** "Black Holes and Entropy." *Physical Review D*, 7, 2333.
- Black holes have entropy
- S_BH = A·k·c³/(4·G·ℏ)
- Information-theoretic connection

## 2.3 Philosophical Questions

### 2.3.1 The Arrow of Time

**Eddington, A. (1928).** "The Nature of the Physical World."
- Named the "arrow of time"
- Entropy defines temporal direction

**Price, H. (1996).** "Time's Arrow and Archimedes' Point."
- Why was entropy low in the past?
- The "past hypothesis" problem

### 2.3.2 Maxwell's Demon

**Maxwell, J.C. (1867).** Original thought experiment.
**Bennett, C. (1982).** "The thermodynamics of computation." *Int. J. Theor. Phys.*
- Information erasure costs entropy
- Demon doesn't violate Second Law

### 2.4.1 The Guna System

**Bhagavad Gita, Chapter 14:**
> "त्रिविधा भवति श्रद्धा देहिनां सा स्वभावजा"
> "Faith (and all qualities) are threefold according to the Gunas"

**Samkhya Karika (Ishvarakrishna):**
The three Gunas:
- Sattva: Lightness, illumination, pleasure
- Rajas: Activity, pain, motion
- Tamas: Heaviness, obscuration, inertia

**Key Properties:**
| Guna | Property | Physical Analog |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| Sattva | Order | Negentropy |
| Rajas | Motion | Kinetic energy |
| Tamas | Inertia | Entropy |
