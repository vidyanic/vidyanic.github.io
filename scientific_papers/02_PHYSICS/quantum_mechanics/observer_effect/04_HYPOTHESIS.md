# 4. Hypothesis

## 4.1 Primary Hypothesis

> **H₀: Consciousness is a fundamental prerequisite for quantum wave function collapse, not reducible to physical processes.**

This is not a claim that consciousness "causes" collapse in a causal-physical sense, but that collapse cannot occur without a conscious observer — consciousness is **irreducible** in the observation chain.

---

## 4.2 Sub-Hypotheses

### H1: Detection Insufficiency

> **H1: A measuring device alone cannot collapse the wave function without a conscious observer.**

**Rationale:**
- Detectors are also quantum systems
- Without observation of detector results, superposition persists
- Delayed choice eraser demonstrates this

**Testable Prediction:**
If detector readings are erased before conscious observation, the wave function should remain uncollapsed (interference pattern preserved).

---

### H2: Heisenberg Cut Indeterminacy

> **H2: The boundary between observer and observed (Heisenberg cut) cannot be defined without invoking consciousness.**

**Rationale:**
- Any physical boundary can be moved (the cut is arbitrary)
- The chain of measurement can extend indefinitely
- Only consciousness provides a definite stopping point

**Testable Prediction:**
No physical experiment can determine where the "measurement" occurs — the cut is always perspective-dependent.

---

### H3: Retrocausal Consistency

> **H3: Conscious observation can affect past quantum events, consistent with delayed choice experiments.**

**Rationale:**
- Wheeler's delayed choice shows future choices affect past patterns
- If consciousness is fundamental, it may be non-local in time
- Backend Framework describes consciousness as outside time (kala)

**Testable Prediction:**
Delayed choice experiments will continue to show retrocausal-like effects.

---

### H4: Backend Architects' Accuracy

> **H4: The Drashta-Drishya framework from Backend Documentation accurately describes the observer-observed relationship in quantum mechanics.**

**Rationale:**
- Drashta (Seer) = Irreducible observer
- Drishya (Seen) = All observed phenomena
- Samyoga (Conjunction) = The measurement event
- This matches quantum findings

**Testable Prediction:**
All quantum observer effects will be consistent with the Drashta-Drishya model.

---

## 4.3 Null Hypothesis

> **H_null: Wave function collapse is entirely physical and does not require conscious observation.**

**Requirements to Accept Null:**
1. A physical definition of "observer" that doesn't require consciousness
2. Experiments showing collapse without any conscious observer
3. Resolution of the Wigner's friend paradox without consciousness

**Current Status:**
No evidence supports the null hypothesis. All attempts to define physical observation have failed.

---

### What Would Disprove Our Hypothesis:

| Prediction | Falsification Condition |
|------------|------------------------|
| H1 | Detector collapses without observation |
| H2 | Physical Heisenberg cut is determined |
| H3 | Delayed choice fails |

### Specific Falsifiable Claims:

3. **If** delayed choice experiments stop showing retrocausal patterns -> **H3 falsified**
4. **If** experiments contradict Drashta-Drishya predictions -> **H4 falsified**
