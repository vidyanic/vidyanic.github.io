## Appendix A: Mathematical Formalism

### A.1 Hilbert Space Basics

```
DEFINITION:
A Hilbert space H is a complete inner product space.

QUANTUM STATES:
|ψ⟩ ∈ H (ket vector)
⟨ψ| ∈ H* (bra vector, dual space)

INNER PRODUCT:
⟨φ|ψ⟩ = complex number
⟨ψ|ψ⟩ = ||ψ||² (norm squared)

OBSERVABLES:
A = A† (Hermitian operators)
Eigenvalues are real
```

### A.2 Measurement Postulates

**Postulate 1 (States):**
The state of a quantum system is described by a vector |ψ⟩ in Hilbert space H.

**Postulate 2 (Observables):**
Physical observables are Hermitian operators A on H.

**Postulate 3 (Measurement Outcomes):**
Possible outcomes are eigenvalues aₙ of A.

**Postulate 4 (Born Rule):**
Probability of outcome aₙ is P(aₙ) = |⟨φₙ|ψ⟩|².

**Postulate 5 (Collapse):**
After measurement, |ψ⟩ -> |φₙ⟩ (the eigenstate of outcome).

### A.3 Density Matrix Formalism

```
PURE STATE:
ρ = |ψ⟩⟨ψ|

MIXED STATE:
ρ = Σᵢ pᵢ|ψᵢ⟩⟨ψᵢ| (where Σpᵢ = 1)

PROPERTIES:
ρ† = ρ (Hermitian)
Tr(ρ) = 1 (normalized)
ρ² = ρ iff pure state

EXPECTATION:
⟨A⟩ = Tr(ρA)
```

## Appendix B: Experimental Details

### B.1 Tonomura Double-Slit (1989)

**Apparatus:**
- Electron gun: 50 keV electrons
- Electron biprism (fine wire as double-slit equivalent)
- Detector: Electron-counting channel plate

**Procedure:**
1. Fire electrons one at a time
2. Record each electron's position
3. Accumulate over time

**Results:**
| Electron Count | Pattern |
|----------------|---------|
| 10 | Random dots |
| 100 | Hints of bands |
| 1,000 | Clear bands |
| 70,000 | Perfect interference |

### B.2 Kim et al. Quantum Eraser (2000)

**Apparatus:**
- BBO crystal (produces entangled photon pairs)
- Signal photon path -> Screen (D0)
- Idler photon path -> Detectors (D1-D4)
- Beam splitters and mirrors

**Key Feature:**
D1, D2 preserve which-path info
D3, D4 erase which-path info
Choice made AFTER signal photon recorded

**Results:**
Sorting D0 data by idler detector shows:
- D1/D2 correlation -> No interference
- D3/D4 correlation -> Interference pattern

### B.3 Proietti et al. Wigner's Friend (2019)

**Apparatus:**
- Entangled photon source
- Two "friends" (measurement stations)
- Two "Wigners" (meta-measurement stations)
- 6-photon setup

**Results:**
- Friends see definite outcomes
- Wigners can verify interference
- Both perspectives consistent with QM
- But not with single objective reality
