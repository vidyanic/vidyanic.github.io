## Abstract

**Scientific Context:** Cancer cells exhibit properties remarkably similar to stem cells: unlimited self-renewal, dedifferentiation, and loss of specialized function. Standard treatment focuses on killing cancer cells, but recurrence suggests a deeper problem.

**Hypothesis:** Cancer is not primarily a "disease to kill" but an IDENTITY CRISIS at the cellular level. Cancer cells are cells that have FORGOTTEN their Svadharma (unique purpose/function) and reverted to an undifferentiated, self-serving state.

**Supporting Evidence:**
1. Cancer cells express embryonic markers
2. Dedifferentiation precedes malignancy
3. Microenvironment can reprogram cancer cells
4. "Sugar coating" (Stanford 2024) helps cancer hide

**Vedic Framework:**
- Svadharma = Cellular function/identity
- Loss of Svadharma = Cancer
- Tamas manifestation = Sugar coating disguise

**Implications:** Treatment should include identity restoration, not just cell death.

**Keywords:** Cancer, stem cells, dedifferentiation, Svadharma, identity, cellular dharma
