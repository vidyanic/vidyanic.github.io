## 1.1 THE DISCOVERY

### The Double Helix (1953)

Watson and Crick published the structure of DNA:
- Two polynucleotide strands
- Wound around each other in a right-handed helix
- Connected by base pairs (A-T, G-C)
- Antiparallel orientation (strands run in opposite directions)
- ~10.5 base pairs per complete turn (B-DNA)

This became the foundation of molecular biology.

### Ida-Pingala (Ancient)

Thousands of years earlier, Vedic texts described:
- Two energy channels (Ida and Pingala)
- Winding around the central Sushumna
- Crossing at chakra points
- Opposite qualities (Moon/Sun, cold/hot, receptive/active)
- Carrying prana and information

**The structural similarity is unmistakable.**
