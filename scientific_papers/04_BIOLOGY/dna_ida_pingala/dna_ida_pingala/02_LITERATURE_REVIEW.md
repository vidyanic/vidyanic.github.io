## 2.1 DNA STRUCTURE DISCOVERY

### Watson & Crick (1953)

- Determined DNA structure using X-ray crystallography
- Double helix model published in Nature
- Base pairing rules (A-T, G-C)
- Antiparallel strand orientation

### Rosalind Franklin & Maurice Wilkins

- X-ray diffraction images crucial to discovery
- Photo 51 showed helical structure

### Three DNA Conformations

| Form | Handedness | bp/turn | Conditions | Discovery |
|------|------------|---------|------------|-----------|
| **B-DNA** | Right | 10.5 | Normal | Watson-Crick 1953 |
| **A-DNA** | Right | 11 | Dehydrated | Franklin 1953 |
| **Z-DNA** | LEFT | 12 | High salt | Rich et al. 1979 |

## 2.2 EPIGENETICS

### Definition

Changes in gene expression WITHOUT changing DNA sequence.

### Mechanisms

1. **DNA Methylation** — Adding methyl groups to cytosine
2. **Histone Modification** — Changing DNA packaging proteins
3. **Non-coding RNA** — Regulatory molecules

### Key Findings

- Epigenetic marks can be inherited (transgenerational)
- Environmental factors affect epigenetics
- Trauma can create epigenetic marks passed to offspring
- Meditation/lifestyle can modify epigenetic patterns

## 2.3 MEDITATION AND DNA

### Scientific Studies

1. **Kaliman et al. (2014)** — Meditation changes DNA methylation
2. **Ornish et al. (2013)** — Lifestyle changes extend telomeres
3. **Bhasin et al. (2013)** — Relaxation response affects gene expression
4. **Bower et al. (2014)** — Yoga alters inflammatory gene expression

### Key Findings

- Mental states DIRECTLY affect DNA
- Stress shortens telomeres
- Meditation extends telomeres
- Practice creates molecular changes
