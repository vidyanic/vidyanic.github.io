# 1. INTRODUCTION

## 1.1 The Plant Consciousness Question

Are plants conscious? Do they experience their existence? This question has profound implications for:
- How we treat the plant kingdom
- Our understanding of consciousness itself
- The validity of the Backend Framework's inclusive model

---

## 1.2 The Traditional View

### 1.2.1 Plants as Machines

For centuries, plants were viewed as:
- Passive, reactive organisms
- Lacking any form of awareness
- Biological machines responding to stimuli
- "Lower" life forms without experience

### 1.2.2 The Consciousness Barrier

Consciousness was reserved for:
- Organisms with nervous systems
- Animals showing behavior complexity
- Especially mammals, primates, humans

Plants lacked the "hardware" for awareness — or so it seemed.

---

## 1.3 The Scientific Revolution

### 1.3.1 Plant Behavior Research

Recent discoveries changed everything:

```
PLANT CAPABILITIES:
==================

COMMUNICATION:
• Chemical signaling (VOCs)
• Electrical action potentials
• Underground networks (mycorrhizae)
• Sound/vibration response

LEARNING:
• Habituation (Mimosa)
• Memory (drought conditioning)
• Anticipation (circadian without cues)

DECISION-MAKING:
• Resource allocation
• Defense timing
• Growth direction choices
```

### 1.3.2 The "Wood Wide Web"

Suzanne Simard's discovery:
- Trees communicate via fungal networks
- Share resources with kin
- Warn neighbors of threats
- Old "mother trees" nurture seedlings
