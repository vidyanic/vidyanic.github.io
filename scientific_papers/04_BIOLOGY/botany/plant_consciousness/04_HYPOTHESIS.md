# 4. HYPOTHESIS

---

## 4.1 Primary Hypothesis

**H1: Plants possess Tamas-dominant consciousness as Sthavara Jivas, experiencing a passive form of awareness that enables learning, memory, communication, and decision-making without centralized neural processing.**

---

## 4.2 Sub-Hypotheses

### H1a: Plants Are Conscious

Plants have subjective experience, though qualitatively different from animal consciousness.

**Predictions:**
- Response complexity beyond reflexes (verified)
- Learning and memory present (verified)
- Decision-making observed (verified)

### H1b: Consciousness is Tamas-Dominant

Plant consciousness is characterized by maximum Tamas, minimal Rajas, minimal Sattva.

**Predictions:**
- Passive, slow, stable awareness
- No seeking behavior (immobility)
- Patient, enduring quality
- Dream-like rather than wakeful

### H1c: Distributed Processing is Valid

Consciousness doesn't require central brain; distributed processing suffices.

**Predictions:**
- Information integration without brain (verified)
- Coordinated whole-organism response (verified)
- Root tips function like neurons (verified)

### H1d: Plant Stage is Essential

Plant existence teaches specific lessons for Jiva development.

**Predictions:**
- Post-plant Jivas have patience capacity
- Pre-plant (mineral) Jivas lack it
- Plants display patience-related adaptations

---

## 4.3 Null Hypotheses

### H0a: Plants Are Not Conscious

All plant responses are mechanical reflexes without any subjective experience.

**If true:**
- No learning or memory (not observed)
- No decision-making (not observed)
- Pure stimulus-response (not observed)

### H0b: Consciousness Requires Brain

Only centralized neural processing generates consciousness.

**If true:**
- Plants would show no integration (not true)
- No coordinated responses (not true)
- No "plant intelligence" phenomena (not true)

## 4.5 Falsification Criteria

### H1 would be falsified if:

1. **No learning at all** — All plant responses purely mechanical
2. **No integration** — Only local, uncoordinated responses
3. **No decision-making** — Random resource allocation
4. **No communication** — No information transfer

Current evidence does not support these null hypotheses.

---
