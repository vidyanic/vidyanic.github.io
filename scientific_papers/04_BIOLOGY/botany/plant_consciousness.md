## 2. Evidence for Plant Intelligence

### 2.1 Memory

**Mimosa pudica experiment (Gagliano, 2014):**
- Dropped plant repeatedly
- Initially closed leaves
- Learned drop was safe â†’ Stopped closing
- Remembered for weeks
- Relearned faster (like neural memory)

### 2.2 Learning

**Pavlovian conditioning in pea plants:**
- Associated fan with light direction
- Later grew toward fan without light
- Classical conditioning without neurons

### 2.3 Communication

**The "Wood Wide Web":**
- Mycorrhizal fungi connect tree roots
- Trees share carbon, water, nutrients
- "Mother trees" support seedlings
- Chemical signals travel through network
- Warning of pest attacks

### 2.4 Decision-Making

**Root foraging:**
- Roots "decide" where to grow
- Assess nutrient distribution
- Avoid competition (kin recognition)
- Optimize for survival

### 2.5 Problem Solving

**Dodder vine:**
- "Smells" potential hosts
- Chooses more nutritious hosts
- Grows toward chosen host
- Decision before contact

## 6. Specific Phenomena

### 6.1 The "Screaming" Plants

**Research (2019):**
- Plants emit ultrasonic sounds when stressed
- Different sounds for drought vs. cutting
- May be detectable by other organisms
- 20-100 kHz range

### 6.2 Kin Recognition

**Plants distinguish relatives:**
- Reduce root competition with kin
- More aggressive with strangers
- "Altruism" toward family

### 6.3 Anesthetic Response

**Plants respond to anesthetics:**
- Venus flytrap doesn't close under anesthesia
- Mimosa doesn't fold
- Pea tendrils stop growing
- Same chemicals that "turn off" human consciousness affect plants
