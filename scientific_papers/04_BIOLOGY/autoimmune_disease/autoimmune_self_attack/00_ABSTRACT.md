# 00. ABSTRACT — Autoimmune Disease as Cellular Identity Crisis

> **"स्वधर्मे निधनं श्रेयः परधर्मो भयावहः"**
> "Svadharme nidhanam shreyah para-dharmo bhayavahah"
> "Better to die in one's own Dharma than to follow another's Dharma, which is dangerous."
> — Bhagavad Gita 3.35

---

## Research Question

Why does the immune system — designed to protect — attack the body's own tissues in autoimmune disease?

## Methodology

Analysis of:
- Immune system as "Dharma enforcement" mechanism
- MHC markers as cellular "Dharmic ID"
- Molecular mimicry as "impostor confusion"
- Comparison with cancer (opposite error)
- Treatment implications

## Key Findings

1. **Autoimmune = Dharma-Vibhrama** (धर्म-विभ्रम/identity confusion)
2. **Immune system is not dysfunctional — it's receiving wrong information**
3. **Healthy cells lose or alter their Dharmic ID markers** -> misidentified as threats
4. **Opposite of cancer:**
   - Cancer = Adharmic cells that HIDE (Maya-Avarana)
   - Autoimmune = Dharmic cells that APPEAR Adharmic
5. **Root causes:** Molecular mimicry, gut permeability, stress, epigenetic changes
6. **Treatment:** Restore cellular Dharmic identity, not suppress immune system

## Conclusions

Autoimmune disease is not immune "dysfunction" — it's immune "miscommunication." The solution is restoring proper cellular identity (Svadharma), not suppressing the enforcement system. This explains why immunosuppressants help symptoms but don't cure.
