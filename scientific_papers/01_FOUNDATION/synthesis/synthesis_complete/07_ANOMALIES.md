## 7.2 Anomalies Explained by Framework

### 7.2.1 Evil and Suffering

**Challenge:** If Brahman is bliss, why does suffering exist?

**Framework Explanation:**
- Brahman as Nirguna is beyond good/evil
- Jagat operates through Gunas including Tamas
- Suffering is Karma feedback, not random
- Liberation (Moksha) transcends suffering

**Status:** ‚úÖ EXPLAINED

---

### 7.2.2 Free Will vs. Determinism

**Challenge:** If Karma determines outcomes, is there free will?

**Framework Explanation:**
- Prarabdha (activated karma) sets conditions
- Kriyamana (new karma) involves choice
- Free will operates within constraints
- Liberation involves transcending both

**Status:** ‚úÖ EXPLAINED

---

### 7.2.3 Individual Consciousness Diversity

**Challenge:** If all is Brahman, why are individuals so different?

**Framework Explanation:**
- Different Guna compositions
- Different Samskaras (accumulated impressions)
- Different Prarabdha (active karma)
- Same source, different expressions

**Status:** ‚úÖ EXPLAINED

## 7.6 Honesty Statement

This framework does not claim to explain everything. It claims to:

1. **Explain more** than materialist alternatives
2. **Be consistent** with known physics
3. **Integrate** ancient and modern knowledge
4. **Be practically useful** for life navigation

**What we don't know:**
- Ultimate "why" questions
- Exact mechanism of some phenomena
- Full details of other Lokas
- Complete Karma accounting system
