## 2.1 CIRCADIAN BIOLOGY

### Nobel Prize Research

**Hall, Rosbash, Young (1984-1990s):**
- Discovered Period gene in fruit flies
- Identified Timeless, Cryptochrome genes
- Mapped transcription-translation feedback loop
- Established molecular clock mechanism

**Key Papers:**
1. Konopka & Benzer (1971) — First clock mutant
2. Rosbash & Hall (1984) — Period gene cloned
3. Young (1994) — Timeless gene discovery

### Molecular Mechanism

```
THE CLOCK CYCLE:
================

CLOCK genes transcribed
        v
CLOCK proteins made
        v
Proteins accumulate
        v
Inhibit own genes
        v
Proteins degrade
        v
Cycle repeats (~24 hours)
```

## 2.2 CHRONOBIOLOGY RESEARCH

### Master Clock Studies

**SCN (Suprachiasmatic Nucleus):**
- Moore & Eichler (1972) — SCN lesions abolish rhythms
- Ralph et al. (1990) — SCN transplant restores rhythm
- Welsh et al. (1995) — Individual SCN neurons oscillate

### Peripheral Clocks

**Every organ has its own clock:**
- Liver (Hepatic), Heart (Cardiac), Muscle (Skeletal)
- Adipose tissue, Gut, Immune cells
- Coordinated by SCN via hormones, temperature, feeding

## 2.3 DISRUPTION RESEARCH

### Shift Work Studies

| Study | Finding |
|-------|---------|
| Schernhammer et al. (2001) | Nurses: increased breast cancer |
| Knutsson (2003) | Shift workers: cardiovascular risk |
| Pan et al. (2011) | Rotating shifts: diabetes risk |

### WHO Classification

**International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC):**
- 2007: Shift work classified as Group 2A — "Probably carcinogenic to humans"

### Jet Lag Research

- Cho et al. (2000) — Chronic jet lag damages temporal lobe
- Davidson et al. (2006) — Repeated jet lag shortens lifespan (mice)
