## 3.1 Circadian Mechanism

### 3.1.1 Molecular Clock

```
CIRCADIAN FEEDBACK LOOP:
========================

Morning:
CLOCK + BMAL1 → Activate PER and CRY genes

Day:
PER and CRY proteins accumulate

Night:
PER/CRY inhibit CLOCK/BMAL1
Gene expression stops

Late night:
PER/CRY degrade

Next morning:
CLOCK/BMAL1 activate again

CYCLE TIME: ~24 hours
```

### 3.1.2 Hierarchy

```
CLOCK HIERARCHY:
================

SCN (Suprachiasmatic Nucleus) - MASTER CLOCK
    |
    +-- Peripheral Clocks (organs, cells)
        |
        +-- Liver clock
        +-- Heart clock
        +-- Skin clock
        +-- etc.

SYNCHRONIZATION:
- Light → SCN (via retina)
- SCN → Peripheral clocks (via hormones, neural)
- Food → Peripheral clocks (independent of SCN)
```

## 3.2 Vedic Kala Framework

### 3.2.1 Time Divisions

```
VEDIC TIME UNITS:
=================

DAILY:
- Ahoratra = 24 hours (day-night)
- Prahara = 3 hours (8 per day)
- Muhurta = 48 minutes (30 per day)
- Nadika = 24 minutes (60 per day)
- Prana = 4 seconds (breath)

MONTHLY:
- Paksha = 15 days (fortnight)
- Masa = 30 days (month)
- Tithi = Lunar day

SEASONAL:
- Rtu = 2 months (6 per year)
- Ayana = 6 months (2 per year)
- Samvatsara = 1 year
```

### 3.2.2 Special Times

**Sandhya (Junction Times):**
- Dawn: Night→Day transition
- Dusk: Day→Night transition
- Noon: Morning→Afternoon (sometimes)

**Brahma Muhurta:**
- ~96 minutes before sunrise
- Considered optimal for spiritual practice
- Modern: Low cortisol, high melatonin transition
