## 2.1 Modern Chronobiology

### 2.1.1 Key Research

| Discovery | Researcher(s) | Year |
|-----------|---------------|------|
| Circadian genes | Hall, Rosbash, Young | 1984-1998 |
| SCN as master clock | Stephan & Zucker | 1972 |
| Light entrainment | Pittendrigh | 1960s |
| Melatonin role | Lerner | 1958 |

### 2.1.2 Nobel Prize Work

2017 Nobel Prize recognized:
- **PER gene** — Period gene cycles ~24h
- **TIM gene** — Helps PER accumulate at night
- **CLOCK/BMAL1** — Activators
- **CRY** — Additional regulators

## 2.2 Types of Rhythms

### 2.2.1 Classification

| Rhythm | Period | Examples |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Circadian | ~24 hours | Sleep-wake, hormones |
| Ultradian | <24 hours | BRAC (90 min), hunger |
| Infradian | >24 hours | Menstrual, seasonal |
| Circannual | ~1 year | Hibernation, mood |

### 2.2.2 What Cycles

- **Hormones:** Cortisol, melatonin, growth hormone
- **Temperature:** Core body temperature
- **Cognition:** Alertness, memory
- **Digestion:** Enzyme production
- **Immunity:** Immune cell activity
