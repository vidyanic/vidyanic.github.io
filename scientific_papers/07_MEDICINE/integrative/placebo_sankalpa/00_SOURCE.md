## 1. What Is the Placebo Effect?

### 1.1 Definition

**Placebo (Latin: "I shall please"):**
- Inert treatment produces real effects
- Patient believes it's effective
- Measurable physiological changes occur
- Not "fake" or imaginary

### 1.2 What Placebos Are

| True Placebo | NOT Placebo |
|--------------|-------------|
| Sugar pill | Active medication |
| Saline injection | Drug injection |
| Sham surgery | Real surgery |
| Fake acupuncture | Real acupuncture |

### 1.3 The Effect Is REAL

**Placebos produce:**
- Pain relief (measurable)
- Immune changes (blood tests)
- Brain changes (fMRI visible)
- Sometimes equal to drugs

---

## 2. Evidence

### 2.1 Placebo Surgery

**Landmark study (2002):**
- Arthroscopic knee surgery vs. sham
- Sham = Incisions only, no procedure
- 2-year follow-up
- **Result: No difference between real and sham**

### 2.2 Open-Label Placebos

**Patients told "this is placebo":**
- Still works
- IBS study: 60% improvement
- Chronic pain: Significant relief
- Cancer fatigue: Improved

**Even knowing it's fake, it works.**

### 2.3 Pain Studies

**Placebo activates:**
- Endorphin system
- Dopamine system
- Same pathways as real painkillers
- Measurable with naloxone blocking

### 2.4 Immune Function

**Placebos can:**
- Increase immune markers
- Reduce inflammation
- Affect white blood cell counts
- Alter cytokine levels

## 3. The Nocebo Effect

### 3.1 Definition

**Nocebo = Negative placebo:**
- Negative expectation -> Negative effect
- Belief in harm causes harm
- Side effects from inert pills
- Death from belief (documented)

### 3.2 Evidence

| Study | Finding |
|-------|---------|
| Voodoo death | Belief can kill (documented) |
| Side effect expectations | More side effects when expected |
| Warnings | Detailed warnings increase side effects |
| Negative diagnoses | Worse outcomes when told "incurable" |

### 3.3 Implication

**Both directions work:**
- Positive belief -> Healing
- Negative belief -> Harm
- Mind affects body BOTH ways
