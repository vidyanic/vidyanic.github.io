## 3.1 Nuclear Transmutation Mechanics

### 3.1.1 Standard Model

```
NUCLEAR TRANSMUTATION:
======================

Input: Element A (protons = Z, neutrons = N)
       |
       v
Process: Nuclear reaction
       - Alpha decay (lose 2p, 2n)
       - Beta decay (n → p or p → n)
       - Fission (split)
       - Fusion (combine)
       |
       v
Output: Element B (protons = Z', neutrons = N')

TRANSMUTATION = CHANGE IN PROTON NUMBER
```

### 3.1.2 Energy Requirements

| Process | Energy Scale | Achievable? |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| Chemical | eV | Yes |
| Nuclear (hot) | MeV | Stars, reactors |
| Nuclear (cold) | keV | LENR? |
| Unknown | ? | Biological? |

## 3.2 LENR Theoretical Models

### 3.2.1 Proposed Mechanisms

1. **Lattice-assisted nuclear reactions**
   - Metal lattice provides environment
   - Quantum tunneling enhanced
   - Collective electron screening

2. **Electron capture**
   - Proton captures electron
   - Forms neutron
   - Enables further reactions

3. **Coherent nuclear reactions**
   - Multiple nuclei act collectively
   - Reduces energy barrier
   - Widom-Larsen theory
