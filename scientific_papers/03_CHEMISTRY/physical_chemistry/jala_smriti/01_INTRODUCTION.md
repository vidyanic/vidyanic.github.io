## 1.1 The Controversy

### 1.1.1 The Claim

Water memory hypothesis proposes:
- Water can retain imprint of substances
- Even after extreme dilution
- Structure carries information
- Can produce biological effects

### 1.1.2 Why Controversial

This challenges conventional chemistry:
- Beyond Avogadro's limit (no molecules remain)
- No known mechanism
- Contradicts standard chemistry
- Difficult to replicate consistently

## 1.3 Water's Unique Properties

### 1.3.1 Already Anomalous

Water is uniquely strange:
- **Expands when freezing** (rare among liquids)
- **Highest surface tension** except mercury
- **Universal solvent** capabilities
- **Highest heat capacity** among common liquids
- **Maximum density at 4°C** (not at 0°C)

### 1.3.2 Hydrogen Bonding

Water molecules:
- Form hydrogen bonds
- Create complex networks
- Constantly reorganizing
- Typical lifetime: ~1 picosecond
