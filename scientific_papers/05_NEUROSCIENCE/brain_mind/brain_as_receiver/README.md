### "If the brain doesn't create consciousness, what does it do?"

It **receives, filters, and translates** consciousness into physical experience.

Think of your brain as a VR headset:
- The headset doesn't CREATE the game ‚Äî it DISPLAYS it
- The headset doesn't CREATE you ‚Äî it CONNECTS you to the game
- Damage the headset -> distorted experience, but YOU still exist

### "But brain damage changes personality!"

Yes ‚Äî just like damaging a VR headset changes your experience. 

If your headset's left eye breaks, you see less. But YOU didn't break ‚Äî the interface did.

Brain damage changes how consciousness is **filtered and expressed**, not consciousness itself.

### "What about anesthesia? It 'turns off' consciousness."

It turns off the **receiver**, not the player.

Unplug your headset, the game screen goes dark. That doesn't mean the game server shut down ‚Äî you just disconnected.

This is why anesthesia is still mysterious ‚Äî we know it works, but not WHY it disconnects the receiver.

### "Prove it."

**NDEs:** People with flat EEGs (zero brain activity) have vivid, verified experiences. If brain GENERATES consciousness, this is impossible. If brain RECEIVES, headset-off doesn't mean player-off.

**Psychedelics:** Reduce brain activity (especially Default Mode Network) but INCREASE experience richness. Generator model: less activity = less experience. Receiver model: reduced filtering = MORE signal gets through.

**Split-brain patients:** When corpus callosum is cut, two "selves" can operate independently. Generator model struggles. Receiver model: one player, two damaged receivers, confusing signals.
