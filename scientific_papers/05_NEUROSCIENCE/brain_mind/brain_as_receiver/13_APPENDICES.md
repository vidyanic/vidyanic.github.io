## Appendix A: Key Case Studies

### A.1 Pam Reynolds (NDE, 1991)

**Context:**
- Underwent hypothermic cardiac arrest surgery
- Brain drained of blood, body cooled to 60Â°F
- No measurable brain activity

**Experience:**
- Observed surgery from ceiling perspective
- Accurately described surgical instruments
- Heard surgeon's conversation
- Met deceased relatives

**Verification:**
- Surgical instruments described matched rare type used
- Surgeon confirmed conversation content
- Details not available through normal perception

**Significance:**
Rich, accurate experience during documented brain shutdown.

---

### A.2 Derek Amato (Acquired Savant, 2006)

**Context:**
- Dove into shallow pool, hit head
- Severe concussion, 35% hearing loss
- No prior musical training

**Experience:**
- Sat at friend's piano days after injury
- Began playing complex compositions
- Sees "black and white squares" in visual field
- Composes without knowledge of music theory

**Neurological:**
- Left frontal/temporal damage
- Enhanced right-brain connectivity

**Significance:**
Complex ability emerged from damage, not learning.

---

### A.3 Anna Katharina Ehmer (Terminal Lucidity, 1922)

**Context:**
- Born with severe developmental disability
- Never spoke in her life
- Bedridden in institution

**Experience:**
- Hours before death, began singing hymns
- Sang clearly and beautifully
- Recognized caretakers
- Died 30 minutes after lucid period

**Significance:**
Damaged brain suddenly producing clear function before death.
