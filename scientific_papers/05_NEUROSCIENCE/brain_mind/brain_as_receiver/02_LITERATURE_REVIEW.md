## 2.1 Historical Development

### 2.1.1 The Transmission Theory

**William James (1898)** â€” *Human Immortality: Two Supposed Objections*

James proposed that the brain doesn't produce consciousness but transmits it:

> "The transmission-theory...is the theory that consciousness is a type of phenomenon which transcends the brain, and that the brain serves to manifest or 'transmit' consciousness rather than produce it."

James noted that all evidence for "production" equally supports "transmission."

## 2.2 Near-Death Experience Research

### 2.2.1 Van Lommel Study (2001)

**Pim van Lommel** â€” Lancet study of 344 cardiac arrest survivors

Key findings:
- 18% reported NDE
- Many NDEs occurred during flat EEG
- Veridical perceptions verified

> "The results of our study showed that consciousness can be experienced independently of a functioning brain."

### 2.2.2 AWARE Study (2014)

**Sam Parnia** â€” Multi-center study of cardiac arrest

Key findings:
- Consciousness can occur after clinical death
- Some verified perceptions during CPR
- Brain activity insufficient for experience

### 2.2.3 Greyson Scale

**Bruce Greyson** â€” Standardized NDE measurement

The Greyson NDE Scale quantifies:
- Cognitive features
- Affective features
- Paranormal features
- Transcendental features
