## 9.3 Objections Addressed

### 9.3.1 "NDEs are just hallucinations"

**Response:** Hallucinations require brain activity. NDEs occur during flat EEG. Also, hallucinations are not veridical — NDEs include verified accurate perceptions.

### 9.3.2 "It's all oxygen deprivation"

**Response:** 
- NDEs occur without hypoxia
- Van Lommel: Blood gases same in NDE vs non-NDE
- Hypoxia causes confusion; NDEs produce clarity
- Hypoxia effects differ qualitatively from NDEs

### 9.3.3 "Maybe the brain wasn't really inactive"

**Response:** 
- EEG flat = No measurable cortical activity
- Cardiac arrest = No blood to brain
- 10-20 seconds to unconsciousness
- If ANY activity sufficient for experience, why don't more have NDEs?

### 9.3.4 "Veridical cases are false memories"

**Response:**
- Immediate reports match later reports
- Details verified independently
- Some details impossible to guess
- AWARE study used hidden targets

### 9.3.5 "Cultural expectation explains features"

**Response:**
- Cross-cultural features remarkably similar
- Children report before cultural exposure
- Core features transcend expectations
- Unexpected elements occur (distressing NDEs)

## 9.4 Limitations

### 9.4.1 What We Don't Know

1. **Exact timing** — When exactly does experience occur?
2. **Selection** — Why do only 18% report NDEs?
3. **Mechanism** — How does consciousness perceive without brain?
4. **Interpretation** — What are beings/places actually?

### 9.4.2 What We Do Know

1. **NDEs occur** — Robust, replicable phenomenon
2. **During arrest** — Timing documented
3. **Veridical** — Some perceptions verified
4. **Transformative** — Lasting positive changes
5. **Universal** — Cross-cultural core features
