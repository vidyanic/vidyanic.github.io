## Research Question

Do Near-Death Experiences (NDEs) provide evidence that consciousness can exist independently of brain function?

---

## Background

Near-Death Experiences are profound subjective experiences reported by individuals who have been clinically dead or very close to death. Common features include out-of-body experiences, tunnel and light phenomena, life review, encounters with deceased persons, and a sense of profound peace.

The materialist paradigm holds that consciousness is produced by the brain. If this is true, NDEs occurring during cardiac arrest (when the brain has no measurable activity) should be impossible.

---
