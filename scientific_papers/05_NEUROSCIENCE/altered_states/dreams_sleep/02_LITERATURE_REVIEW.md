## 2.1 MODERN SLEEP RESEARCH

### Sleep Architecture

**Nathaniel Kleitman & Eugene Aserinsky (1953)**
- Discovered REM (Rapid Eye Movement) sleep
- Noted eye movements correlate with dreaming
- Revolutionized sleep research

**William Dement (1957-1960s)**
- Characterized sleep cycles
- Mapped stage progression
- Established 90-minute cycle

### Sleep Stages (Current Model)

| Stage | EEG Pattern | Duration | Function |
|-------|-------------|----------|----------|
| **Wake** | Beta (13-30 Hz), Alpha (8-12 Hz) | — | Alert/relaxed |
| **N1** | Theta (4-7 Hz) | 5% | Transition |
| **N2** | Theta + spindles, K-complexes | 50% | Light sleep |
| **N3** | Delta (0.5-4 Hz) | 20% | Deep restoration |
| **REM** | Beta-like (desynchronized) | 25% | Dreams, memory |

### Key Discoveries

1. **Sleep cycles** — 4-6 cycles per night, ~90 min each
2. **REM rebound** — Missing REM causes more REM later
3. **Memory consolidation** — Sleep essential for learning
4. **Emotional processing** — Dreams process feelings

## 2.3 DEEP SLEEP MYSTERIES

### What Happens in Deep Sleep?

```
DEEP SLEEP (N3/NREM 3):
=======================

• Delta waves dominant (0.5-4 Hz)
• Minimal sensory processing
• Very hard to awaken
• Limited or no dream recall
• Body restoration maximal
• Brain activity minimal

THE QUESTION:
Where is "you" during this time?
```

### Glymphatic System Discovery

**Maiken Nedergaard (2012-present)**
- Discovered brain "cleaning" system
- Cerebrospinal fluid flushes toxins
- Only active during deep sleep
- Clears Alzheimer's-associated proteins

### The Consciousness Gap

- Brain activity minimal in deep sleep
- No experience typically reported
- Yet personal identity continues
- Something persists — but what?

## 2.4 MANDUKYA UPANISHAD

### The Text

One of the shortest and most profound Upanishads (12 verses). Analyzes OM (AUM) as the sound of consciousness and maps it to four states.

### The Four States

**Verse 3-7 Summary:**

| State | Body | Awareness | Experience |
|-------|------|-----------|------------|
| **Jagrat** | Sthula (gross) | External, 19 channels | World of objects |
| **Swapna** | Sukshma (subtle) | Internal, 19 channels | Mind-created world |
| **Sushupti** | Karana (causal) | Unified, no objects | Bliss, undifferentiated |
| **Turiya** | None (witness) | Pure awareness | No objects, no subject split |

### Gaudapada's Karika

Commentary on Mandukya (~6th century CE):
- Extended philosophical analysis
- Argued all states are "dream-like"
- Only Turiya is ultimately real
- Influenced Advaita Vedanta
