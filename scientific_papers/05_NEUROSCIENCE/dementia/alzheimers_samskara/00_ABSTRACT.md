## Title

**Alzheimer's Disease as Samskara Overload: A Backend Framework Analysis of Memory System Failure**

---

## Abstract

This paper proposes that Alzheimer's disease is fundamentally a Samskara Overload — a failure of the brain's memory processing and clearing systems, leading to emergency deletion protocols that preserve core identity at the expense of recent memories.

We demonstrate that:

1. **Samskaras = Memory imprints** — Every experience creates neural and epigenetic marks
2. **Normal processing** — Sleep, meditation, forgiveness clear and consolidate
3. **Overload** — More accumulation than clearing leads to system stress
4. **Emergency protocol** — Delete newest to preserve oldest/core
5. **Alzheimer's pattern** — Recent memory first, procedural last

This model explains:
- Why memory loss follows a specific pattern (newest to oldest)
- Why procedural memory (skills) is preserved longest
- Why sleep deprivation is a major risk factor
- Why meditation and mindfulness may be protective
- Why current treatments targeting plaques fail
