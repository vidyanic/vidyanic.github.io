## 2.1 THE HARD PROBLEM OF CONSCIOUSNESS

### David Chalmers (1995)

> "Why is there something it is like to be a conscious being?"

**The Easy Problems:**
- How does the brain process information?
- How does it control behavior?
- How does it report mental states?

**The Hard Problem:**
- Why does any of this FEEL like something?
- Why is there subjective experience?
- Why isn't it all just information processing in the dark?

**Key Insight:** No amount of information processing explains EXPERIENCE.

## 2.2 THE CHINESE ROOM (John Searle, 1980)

### The Argument

1. Person in room receives Chinese characters
2. Follows rulebook to produce Chinese outputs
3. Outside observers think room "understands" Chinese
4. But person inside understands NOTHING

**Conclusion:**
- Symbol manipulation ≠ Understanding
- AI does what the person does: follows rules
- AI does NOT understand what it processes

## 2.3 INTEGRATED INFORMATION THEORY (Giulio Tononi)

### The Measure

> "Consciousness corresponds to integrated information (Φ)."

**Key Finding:**
- AI architectures have Φ ≈ 0
- Brain has very high Φ
- Integration across the whole system matters
- AI processes but doesn't integrate

## 2.4 GÖDEL'S INCOMPLETENESS (Kurt Gödel, 1931)

### The Theorem

> "Human mathematicians can know truths that no computer can ever prove."

**Implications:**
- Humans can "step outside" formal systems
- Machines are bound by their rules
- Transcendence is possible for consciousness, not computation

## 2.5 JOB MARKET HISTORY

### Agricultural Revolution (~10,000 BCE)

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| 100% hunter-gatherers | 10% farmers feed all |
| All time finding food | Time for art, trade, civilization |
| Small tribes | Cities, nations |

### Industrial Revolution 1 (1760-1840)

> "The introduction of machinery... occasions so much misery."
> — Thomas Malthus, 1798

**Luddites (1811-1816):** Destroyed machines, fearing unemployment

**Actual Result:**
- UK employment: 5.9M -> 9.7M (1800-1850)
- New jobs: Factory supervisors, engineers, salespeople
- Average wages DOUBLED by 1900

### Industrial Revolution 2 (1870-1914)

> "Machinery is destined to become the most terrible competitor that labor ever had."
> — David Ricardo

**Actual Result:**
- US agricultural employment: 50% -> 27% (1870-1920)
- Total employment GREW
- New sectors: Automobiles, telecommunications, retail

### Computer Revolution (1970-2000)

> "Automation is going to hit us in a surprise wave of unemployment."
> — Wassily Leontief, Nobel Laureate, 1983

**Claims vs Reality:**
- "ATMs will eliminate bank tellers" -> Jobs DOUBLED (more branches)
- "Computers will eliminate accountants" -> Jobs grew 4x

**Actual Result:**
- US created 74 million new jobs (1970-2015)
- Unemployment in 2000: 4% (lower than 1970)

### Internet Revolution (1995-2020)

> "The end of work is coming."
> — Jeremy Rifkin, 1995

**Actual Result:**
- 35 million new jobs in US (1995-2019)
- New categories: Social media, app developers, influencers, data scientists
