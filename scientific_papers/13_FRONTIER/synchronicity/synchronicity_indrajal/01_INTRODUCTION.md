## 1.1 The Phenomenon of Synchronicity

"Meaningful coincidences" — events that seem connected by meaning rather than causality. Examples:

- Thinking of someone, they call
- Reading about a topic, it appears everywhere
- Dreaming of an event, it happens
- Meeting the right person at the right time

---

## 1.2 Jung's Definition

Carl Jung (1952) coined "synchronicity":

> "The simultaneous occurrence of two meaningfully but not causally connected events."

Jung saw synchronicity as evidence for a deeper ordering principle in reality — what he called the "collective unconscious."
