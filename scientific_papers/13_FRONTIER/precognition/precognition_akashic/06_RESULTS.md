## 6.1 Laboratory Evidence

### 6.1.1 Bem Studies (2011)

```
BEM'S 9 EXPERIMENTS:
===================

METHODOLOGY:
• Retroactive priming
• Precognitive avoidance
• Retroactive recall facilitation

RESULTS:
• 8 of 9 experiments significant
• Combined p < 0.001
• Effect size: Cohen's d ≈ 0.22

CONTROVERSY:
• Replication attempts mixed
• Methodological critiques
• But meta-analyses support effect
```

### 6.1.2 Presentiment Studies

| Study | Measure | Finding |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Radin (1997) | Skin conductance | 2-6 sec anticipation |
| Bierman (2000) | fMRI | Brain anticipates |
| McCraty (2004) | Heart rhythm | Heart "knows" first |
| Mossbridge meta (2012) | 26 studies | Significant aggregate |

### 6.1.3 Effect Sizes

```
TYPICAL EFFECT SIZES:
====================

• Presentiment: d ≈ 0.20-0.30
• Bem experiments: d ≈ 0.22
• Meta-analyses: Small but consistent

COMPARISON:
• Similar to many accepted psychological effects
• Smaller than placebo but real
• Consistent across studies
```
