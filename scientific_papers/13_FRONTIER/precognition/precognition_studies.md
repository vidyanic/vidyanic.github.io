## 2. The Bem Study (2011)

### 2.1 The Research

**Dr. Daryl Bem, Cornell University:**
- 9 experiments
- 1,000+ subjects
- Standard psychological paradigms
- Run "backward in time"

### 2.2 The Method

**Example experiment:**
- Subject chooses between two curtains
- AFTER choice, computer randomly places image
- Result: Erotic images "chosen" at 53.1% (chance = 50%)
- Highly significant (p < 0.01)

### 2.3 Results

**8 of 9 experiments showed significant effects.**

| Experiment | Effect | Significance |
|------------|--------|--------------|
| Precognitive detection | 53.1% | p < 0.01 |
| Retroactive priming | Significant | p < 0.05 |
| Retroactive habituation | Significant | p < 0.05 |
| etc. | | |

### 2.4 Controversy

**Criticized for:**
- Optional stopping
- Multiple analyses
- Effect size small

**But:**
- Published in mainstream journal
- Standard methods used
- Replications attempted worldwide

## 3. Presentiment Studies

### 3.1 What Is Presentiment?

**Pre-stimulus response:**
- Body reacts BEFORE seeing stimulus
- Especially emotional/arousing stimuli
- Measured via skin conductance, heart rate
- Subject doesn't consciously know

### 3.2 Key Research

**Dean Radin (IONS):**
- Subjects shown random images
- Some calm, some arousing
- Physiological measures taken
- Body reacts differently BEFORE arousing vs. calm images

### 3.3 Results

**Meta-analysis (Mossbridge et al., 2012):**
- 26 studies analyzed
- Significant effect
- Body anticipates future
- Effect size small but consistent
