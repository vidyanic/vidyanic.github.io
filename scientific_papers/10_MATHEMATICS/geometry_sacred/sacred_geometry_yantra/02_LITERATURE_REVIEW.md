# 02. Literature Review: Mathematical and Vedic Sources

## 2.1 Mathematical Literature

### 2.1.1 Golden Ratio History

**Euclid (300 BCE)**
*Elements* — First rigorous definition of extreme and mean ratio (golden ratio)

**Fibonacci (Leonardo of Pisa, 1202)**
*Liber Abaci* — Introduced the Fibonacci sequence to Western mathematics

**Luca Pacioli (1509)**
*De Divina Proportione* — Illustrated by da Vinci, called it "Divine Proportion"

**Johannes Kepler (1611)**
Studied φ in planetary orbits and biological forms

### 2.1.2 Modern Research

**Mario Livio (2002)**
*"The Golden Ratio: The Story of Phi"*
- Comprehensive history and analysis
- Debunks some myths, confirms genuine occurrences

**H.E. Huntley (1970)**
*"The Divine Proportion"*
- Mathematical analysis
- Aesthetic applications

**Ian Stewart (1995)**
*"Nature's Numbers"*
- Mathematics in natural patterns
- Explains biological Fibonacci
