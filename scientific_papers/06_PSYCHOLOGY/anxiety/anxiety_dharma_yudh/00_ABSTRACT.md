## Title

**Anxiety as Internal Dharma Yudh: Reframing Anxiety Disorders as Svadharma-Paradharma Conflict**

---

## Abstract

This paper proposes that anxiety disorders are fundamentally manifestations of Dharma Yudh (धर्म युद्ध) — an internal war between one's Svadharma (authentic nature/purpose) and Paradharma (imposed or adopted roles inconsistent with one's nature).

We demonstrate that:

1. **Anxiety = Dharma conflict** — The body's response to continuous self-betrayal
2. **Chronic fight-or-flight** — Results from living against one's nature
3. **Arjuna's Vishada** — First documented case study of anxiety disorder
4. **Treatment failure** — Medications suppress symptoms without resolving the conflict
5. **True healing** — Requires Dharma realignment, not symptom suppression

This model explains:
- Why anxiety is epidemic in modern societies (Dharma-denying cultures)
- Why medications have limited long-term efficacy
- Why purpose-driven individuals rarely have chronic anxiety
- Why existential therapy often outperforms pharmacology

## Research Question

Is chronic anxiety a symptom of living in conflict with one's Svadharma (authentic nature and purpose)?

---

## Hypothesis

**H₁:** Anxiety disorders arise primarily from Svadharma-Paradharma conflict. Resolution requires Dharma realignment rather than (or in addition to) symptom suppression.
